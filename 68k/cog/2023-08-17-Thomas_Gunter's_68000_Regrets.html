Thomas Gunter's 68000 Regrets

During the Motorola 68000 Oral History Panel from the Computer History Museum, Thomas Gunter, the director of the 68000 program, noted a few regrets around the development of the 68000 (CHM 9):

  • Should have gone with a CMOS implementation earlier
  • Didn't get a floating point unit until the 68020
  • "We created a monster in terms of all of the addressing modes"

That last one is kind of an interesting point:

As we went up the chain a little bit to 68010, 68020, we created a monster in terms of all the addressing modes that we had. We thought that adding more addressing modes was the way you made a machine more powerful, totally contrary to the principle of RISC later.

This is interesting to me because I find, as I'm programming the 68000, it has exactly the addressing modes I would expect. They're very idiomatic to someone who has experience with C or C++. Perhaps he was speaking as a designer, the logic needed to implement them all must have been challenging. All that said, I find them to be just enough (NXP 2-4):

Most of these I wouldn't want to live without. Perhaps the PC-relative addressing modes weren't absolutely necessary but, with a large address space, being able to load relocatable code is very useful.
